Learn How to Make Pasta

Making fresh pasta from scratch is easier than you might think. Follow our simple recipe to create delicious homemade pasta in no time.

Get Started

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.
  2. Create a well in the center of the flour mixture and add the eggs and olive oil.
  3. Using a fork, whisk the eggs and oil together, gradually incorporating the flour from the sides of the well.

    4. Tips for Making Pasta from Scratch

    • Use the right flour: The best flour for making pasta is “00” flour, which is finely ground and has a high protein content. If you can’t find this type of flour, you can also use all-purpose flour.
    • Measure accurately: To ensure the perfect consistency of your pasta dough, it’s important to measure your ingredients accurately. Use a kitchen scale if possible.
    • Add eggs at room temperature: Using eggs that are at room temperature will help them combine better with the flour and will make it easier to create a smooth and elastic dough.
    • Knead the dough well: Kneading the dough is important to activate the gluten in the flour and to create a smooth, elastic dough. Be sure to knead the dough for at least 10-15 minutes, until it’s smooth and pliable.
    • Rest the dough: After kneading the dough, let it rest for at least 30 minutes before rolling it out. This will allow the gluten to relax and make the dough easier to work with.
    • Roll the dough thinly: For the best pasta texture, roll the dough out as thinly as possible. You can use a pasta machine or a rolling pin to achieve this.
    • Cut the pasta into the desired shape: Once the pasta is rolled out, cut it into the desired shape using a pasta cutter or a knife. You can make spaghetti, fettuccine, lasagna sheets, or any other shape you like.
    • Cook the pasta in boiling salted water: To cook the pasta, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and add the pasta. Cook the pasta until it’s al dente, or firm to the bite, which usually takes 2-3 minutes for fresh pasta.
    • Serve immediately: Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and toss it with your desired sauce. Homemade pasta is best served immediately, while it’s still warm and fresh.

Conclusion

With these tips and techniques, you should now have the confidence to make delicious pasta from scratch right in your own kitchen. Remember to use high-quality ingredients and take your time when rolling out and cutting the dough. Once you've mastered the basics, feel free to experiment with different shapes, flavors, and sauces to create your own signature dishes.
